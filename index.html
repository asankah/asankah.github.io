Draining Our Intellectual Catchment Areas

Last updated on July 4, 2025 #Culture

Intellectual catchment areas are collaborative ecosystems between universities and industries that attract and channel talent and ideas. The United States became a hub of intellectual capital after World War II, thanks to a combination of industrial growth around universities, significant research funding, and favorable immigration policies.

However, the current political climate is jeopardizing these ecosystems. Continued threats against universities and immigration could undo a century of progress, ultimately depleting the nation's source of creativity and innovation.
